# The Origin Story

## The Hook
Why does Flo live in a van? It started during COVID.

## The Setup
Flo is locked up in her apartment. Playing video games. Doing a little remote work. Stuck. Not depressed exactly — just... frozen. The world stopped and she stopped with it.

Days blur together. Same apartment. Same screen. Same nothing.

## The Moment
It's Thanksgiving. Flo is alone (or might as well be). She sees a picture on Instagram — someone made a cake out of three cakes stacked on top of each other. For some reason, this is the thing she decides to do today. She goes to Costco. Buys three cakes. Stacks them on top of each other in her apartment.

She stands there looking at this ridiculous triple cake and something clicks. Not a dramatic epiphany. Not a movie moment. Just a quiet "what am I doing?"

## The Path to the Van
It wasn't a straight line.

First she was looking at land, thinking about building a tiny house. She'd already bought a brand new pickup truck — she loved that truck. She hadn't owned a car in years, used to rent cars just for road trips.

Then COVID hit and she started watching van life videos. Obsessively. She looked at getting a camper shell for the truck, but the whole idea of parking, getting out, walking around to the back, climbing into the camper — terrible. Versus just... parking and jumping in bed. That's when she knew it had to be a van.

Trading in the truck was brutal. She'd had it six months. The dealer basically said "you have to pay us to take it" because she'd paid way more than it was worth six months later. She ate the loss.

Then she bought the van. And everything changed.

## Why It Works
- This is how real life changes actually start — not with a dramatic speech, but with a triple Costco cake
- COVID lockdown is universally relatable. Everyone had their version of this
- The cake detail is specific, weird, and human. It's the kind of thing you can't make up.
- It reframes the whole channel: Flo isn't performing van life. She's escaping something. The van was the solution, not the goal.
- Sets up the van build series naturally — we know WHY she's building it

## Tone
Not sad. Not dramatic. Almost funny. The humor of realizing you've been sleepwalking through life because you're stacking three Costco cakes at Thanksgiving. The drone doesn't exist yet in this timeline — this is pre-drone Flo. Shot like old phone footage or apartment security cam aesthetic.

## When to Release
This could be a flashback episode after the audience already knows and loves van-life Flo. More powerful that way — you already know this girl is thriving, and now you see where she started.
