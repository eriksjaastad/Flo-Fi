# The Pilot: Introducing the Drone

## The Hook
The first and last time Flo uses her phone to film anything.

## Timeline
Post-college. Flo moved away from home. Living in an apartment far from her mom. Pre-COVID, pre-van. This is the very beginning.

## The Scene
Flo is in her apartment. Just moved, probably a little lonely. She's been building something on her kitchen table — parts, wires, tools scattered around. She picks up her phone — the only time we ever see her holding a camera.

"Mom, I want to show you something I built."

She points it at the drone sitting on the table. Maybe we see mom's face on the phone screen — not clearly, just a reaction. Wonder. Maybe a little worry. Definitely pride.

"I know you worry. So now you can always see where I am. I'll leave it on whenever I'm out. You can watch whenever you want."

She turns the drone on. It lifts off from the table. Wobbles a little (it's new, she just built it).

Cut to: the drone's perspective, looking at Flo. She waves. She smiles. She puts her phone down.

We never see her phone again. Everything from this moment forward is the drone's feed. Every video is her talking to mom without needing to stop and dial. The audience figures this out slowly. Nobody explains it. You just eventually realize — oh. She built this for her mom. Every single video is for her mom.

## Why It Works
- Establishes the entire format of the channel in 30 seconds
- Explains why the camera exists without ever breaking the fourth wall again
- The audience understands immediately: this is mom's feed, we're just watching
- The phone-to-drone camera switch is a visual handoff that the audience will remember
- It's sweet without being cheesy — she built a drone so she wouldn't have to make awkward phone calls
- Sets the tone: Flo solves problems by building things
- The drone is born out of love and homesickness, not tech ambition
- By the time she gets the van, the drone is already her constant companion. Her mom has been watching the whole time.

## Story Timeline After This
1. Pilot: drone intro (this episode) — apartment, pre-everything
2. Apartment life / COVID lockdown / the triple cake moment
3. Van research, truck purchase, truck trade-in
4. Van build series
5. First trip
6. Everything else (sea turtles, etc.)

## Technical Notes
- First half is phone footage aesthetic (handheld, vertical, slightly shaky)
- Second half switches to drone perspective (smooth, aerial, wider)
- The transition between the two is THE moment — the birth of the show's visual language
- Keep it short. 30-60 seconds max. This is a cold open, not a full episode.

## When to Release
This is literally the first thing anyone sees. Episode zero. The pilot.
