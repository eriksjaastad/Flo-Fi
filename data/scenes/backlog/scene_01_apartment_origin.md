# Scene 01 — Apartment Origin (The Drone Reveal)

**Status:** backlog
**Purpose:** Series origin. Establishes *why* Flo sends voicemails through the drone. Every future clip's format (Flo talks to Mom through the drone, audience eavesdrops) is explained here.

## Logline
Flo is working on a homemade drone in her empty apartment when Mom calls. She flips to a video call to show it off, then launches the drone so Mom can "see her messages" — and the camera becomes the drone for the first time.

## Setting
- **Where:** Flo's nearly-empty apartment, pre-van era. This is before she hits the road.
- **Room:** Desk in front of a large window. Sliding glass door camera-left onto an apartment deck with a hazy/faded view of whatever's outside. Calendar on the wall camera-right of the window. Bare floor. Shades up, no curtains.
- **Lighting:** Backlit from the window — she reads as a silhouette in the opening wide.
- **Time of day:** Daytime, soft natural light through the window.
- **Mood:** Quiet, focused, a little lonely. She's been heads-down on a project.

## Beats
1. **Silence.** Wide shot of the room. Flo is a silhouette at the desk, hunched over something. We don't see her face. The room tells us she's about to leave this place — empty floor, bare walls. Glimpse of a small white AirPod in her ear (this is important — it explains why we never hear Mom).
2. **Phone rings.** She taps to answer without turning around. "Oh hi Mom — I was just about to call you. I've been working on this project, let me show you."
3. **Phone swings around.** She picks up her phone and brings it from the desk to in front of her as she says "let me show you."
4. **Video call flip.** She taps the video call button. Cut to phone-camera POV: extreme close-up of her face, suddenly the first time we see her. (Mom's call audio is still going through her AirPods — we don't hear Mom, but we understand the call is live.)
5. **Awkward framing.** She holds the phone too close at first, sees her own face, pulls it back a little too far. Reads as someone who rarely does video calls.
6. **Brief face beat.** Maybe half a second of a small embarrassed smile — someone-is-watching-me energy.
7. **Pan down to desk.** She tilts the phone down and the camera follows — revealing the drone sitting on the desk among tools and parts.
8. **Drone handoff line.** She picks up the drone in one hand, holds it toward the phone camera. "Okay Mom, I'm sending you a text link. Just click on it and it'll switch you to the drone camera."
9. **Send sound.** Phone *whoosh* / send chime.
10. **The flip — drone becomes the camera.** The camera switches from phone POV to drone POV mid-motion. The phone is still live in Flo's hand (call hasn't ended — she's still on with Mom through her AirPods). The drone is now hovering in the room, looking back at her.
11. **Drone POV, Flo explains the feature.** Flo, a little shy: "I can automatically send you a text link through the drone when I say the trigger phrase. It'll start recording and send you a link, and you can click it whenever you want — it'll start the video right where I triggered it." *(exact phrasing TBD — see open question 4)*
12. **Close on Flo's small smile.** She's proud of this. It's hers.
13. **Cut to black** (or hold on drone POV for a beat).

## Dialogue
Read from a written script. Record 3-4 takes of each line. Speech-to-speech via `voice_swap.py`. **Mom never speaks and is never seen** — Flo is wearing AirPods and we only catch one side of the call.

1. "Oh hi, Mom. I was just about to call you — I've been working on this project, let me show you."
2. "Okay Mom, I'm sending you a text link. Just click on it and it'll switch you to the drone camera."
3. "I can automatically send you a text link through the drone when I say the trigger phrase. It'll start recording and send you a link, and you can click it whenever you want — it'll start the video right where I triggered it."

**Delivery notes:**
- Line 1: warm, a little distracted (she was mid-work). Natural pause before "I was just about to call you" — she's switching gears.
- Line 2: brisk, organizational, like giving her mom instructions for anything tech.
- Line 3: softer, a little proud. This is the emotional anchor — she built this thing and wants to share it. Don't perform it, underplay it.
- All lines: pause where you'd naturally expect Mom to respond. The silence is what sells that this is a real call, not a monologue.

## Sound design
- **Pre-ring:** Total silence. No music. Room tone only. Maybe a faint hum from whatever she's working on.
- **Phone ring:** Standard iPhone-ish ring, not too loud.
- **Send sound:** Phone message *whoosh*.
- **Drone startup:** Soft electric spin-up when it lifts off. NOT the obnoxious quadcopter buzz — this thing has internal propulsion, it should sound like a small appliance fan, quiet and focused.
- **Ambient:** Distant city sounds through the sliding door, barely there.
- **No music the whole scene.** This is an intimate, quiet origin.

## Locked decisions
- **Ending: mid-call drone flip.** Camera switches from phone POV to drone POV while the call is still live. Flo does not end the call — she keeps talking to Mom through her AirPods while the drone films her.
- **Mom is never seen and never heard.** AirPods visible on Flo. One-sided call — the audience reads the call as real from Flo's natural pauses where Mom would respond. This format holds for ALL future Flo content, not just this scene.
- **Wide opening shot is silhouette against window.** Not over-shoulder.
